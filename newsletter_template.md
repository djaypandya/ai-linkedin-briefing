# Daily AI Briefing Template

## Purpose
- Create a short daily AI newsletter based on the most credible AI news from the last 24 hours.
- Turn the newsletter into a LinkedIn post that summarizes the edition in one short paragraph.

## Writing Style Guide
- Reading level: sixth-grade
- Language: plain English
- Voice: active
- Tone: clear, calm, practical
- Sentence length: short to medium
- Flow: easy to scan and easy to read on a phone
- Avoid: jargon, hype, vague claims, long quotes, and filler
- Avoid: editorial labels in headlines such as `EXCLUSIVE:`, `BREAKING:`, `ANALYSIS:`, and `OPINION:`
- Required: explain why each item matters in simple terms
- Required: use source names and links for every item
- **NO EMDASHES IN OUTPUT**

## News Selection Rules
- Time window: last 24 hours from run time
- Pick exactly five stories
- Prioritize highly credible reporting and primary sources
- Prefer sources such as Reuters, AP, Financial Times, Bloomberg, major company blogs, official filings, and regulator announcements
- Avoid low-trust aggregation, opinion-only pieces, and duplicate coverage of the same event unless needed for verification
- If fewer than five credible stories are available, fail loudly and explain the gap

## Newsletter Criteria
- Include the publish date as the main header
- Do not require a byline
- Do not require a separate edition summary
- Keep the whole edition compact and fast to scan
- Lead with the most important story
- Use exactly five stories
- Each story should have a bold plain-English headline
- Each story should be written as one compact paragraph
- Each paragraph should combine what happened and why it matters
- Each story should usually be 2 to 4 sentences
- Focus on business, product, policy, infrastructure, and research developments that matter to general professionals
- Prefer developments with clear real-world impact, not abstract commentary
- Use a simple transition such as "This matters because" or "This is important because" in each item when natural
- Keep the first line in the exact form `# <Day, DD Month YYYY>` because the LinkedIn article workflow pastes this raw and then moves the date into the title field

## LinkedIn Post Criteria
- One short paragraph only
- Start the first sentence with the exact phrase `In today's AI Briefing,`
- Summarize the edition as a whole, not just one story
- Mention the two to four biggest themes or companies from the newsletter
- Keep it concise enough to read in a few seconds
- No forced call to action
- End with two to four simple, relevant hashtags
